# Exodus — book context for review

- **Source**: Westminster Leningrad Codex (WLC) / Unicode Hebrew Bible (UHB). 40 chapters.
- **Form**: Narrative and law. The narrative (1–18) tells Israel's liberation from Egypt; Sinai theophany (19–24); tabernacle instructions and construction (25–31; 35–40); golden calf episode (32–34).
- **Date**: Final form likely post-exilic (6th–5th cent. BC), though core traditions are much older.
- **Original audience**: Israel's identity-forming narrative — who they are as a people, and how they are to worship.

## Key translation issues

1. **The divine name YHWH**: Render as "the LORD" (small caps) consistently per project convention — do not substitute "Yahweh" or "God" unless context demands.
2. **שְׁמוֹת / שֵׁם** ("name"): The book's Hebrew title is "Names." The theme of divine naming runs throughout (3:13–15, God's name revealed). Keep "name" — do not paraphrase.
3. **Exodus 3:14 — אֶהְיֶה אֲשֶׁר אֶהְיֶה** ("I am who I am / I will be who I will be"): The divine self-disclosure. Both "I am" and "I will be" are grammatically defensible (imperfect can be present or future). Keep "I am who I am" as the primary rendering; footnote the future option.
4. **Cultic/tabernacle vocabulary** (chs. 25–31; 35–40): Terms for the tabernacle structure and furnishings must be rendered accurately and consistently — מִשְׁכָּן (tabernacle/dwelling), אֹהֶל מוֹעֵד (tent of meeting), אָרוֹן (ark), כַּפֹּרֶת (mercy seat/cover/atonement cover). Establish terms early and keep them throughout.
5. **עֶבֶד / עֲבֹדָה** ("slave/servant / service/slavery/worship"): The same root covers Israel's slavery to Pharaoh and Israel's "worship/service" to God. The wordplay is significant: 4:23 "Let my son go that he may serve (עָבַד) me" vs. their service to Pharaoh. Preserve the root connection where possible.
6. **The plagues**: Each plague account uses formulaic language. Maintain the formulaic consistency ("Moses stretched out his staff…").
7. **כָּבֵד / כָּבוֹד** ("heavy / glory"): Pharaoh's "hardened heart" uses כָּבֵד (heavy) — the same root as YHWH's "glory" (כָּבוֹד). This wordplay should be noted; English "hardened" and "glory" lose the connection, but both are standard.

## Style / register

- Narrative sections: terse, direct Hebrew storytelling prose.
- Legal/cultic sections (Decalogue, covenant code, tabernacle): formulaic, repetitive, precise. Preserve the repetition in the tabernacle construction accounts — "as the LORD commanded Moses" is repeated formulaically.
- Theophanic passages (19:16–25; 24:9–11; 33:17–23; 34:5–8) are sublime and awe-inspiring — render with full weight.

## Known pitfalls

- **Inconsistent tabernacle vocabulary** across chapters 25–31 and 35–40. The construction narrative repeats the instructions almost verbatim — they must match.
- **Softening "hardening of Pharaoh's heart"** — all three verbs (קָשָׁה, חָזַק, כָּבֵד) should be rendered distinctly where they appear.
- **Missing the servitude/worship wordplay** in the Exodus narrative.
