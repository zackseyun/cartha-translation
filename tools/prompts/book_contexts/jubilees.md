# Jubilees — book context for review

- **Work**: The Book of Jubilees (*Sefer ha-Yovelim*)
- **Form**: Retelling of Genesis 1 through Exodus 24, framed as God's dictation to Moses via an angel of the presence on Mount Sinai. 50 chapters, organized by Jubilees (49-year cycles).
- **Language situation**: Composed in Hebrew; Hebrew fragments at Qumran (4Q176, 4Q216–224, 4Q482–483; 1QS parallels). Our complete text survives in **Ge'ez (Classical Ethiopic)** — the Ethiopic church preserved it as canonical. A Latin version survives for parts. Syriac quotations.
- **Source for this edition**: Charles 1902 Ethiopic text (primary); Ge'ez Qumran fragments for text-critical checks.
- **Date**: 2nd c. BC, likely ca. 170–150 BC (Maccabean or slightly pre-Maccabean). Qumran community held it as authoritative.
- **Original audience**: Second Temple Jews committed to a 364-day solar calendar (vs. the lunar calendar of the Jerusalem priesthood) and to stricter Torah observance. Jubilees rewrites Genesis-Exodus to push halakhic positions.

## What matters most for translation

1. **Preserve the didactic-legal register alongside the narrative.** Jubilees is not just retelling stories; it is making legal arguments by narrating. The haggadic expansions carry halakhic intent — render them with the same weight as the narrative.
2. **The 364-day calendar is a polemic.** All date formulae (sabbaths, festivals, jubilee years) are exact and deliberate. Do not smooth or approximate date calculations.
3. **Angelic mediation**: the Angel of the Presence dictates to Moses. This framing is theological — the authority claim runs through every passage. Preserve the angelic-dictation formula.
4. **Patriarchal speeches and prayers** should have an elevated register — these are not casual conversation but formal testamentary speech.
5. **Legal expansions** (e.g., blood-atonement rationale for circumcision; Sabbath rules; forbidden marriages): render the legal argumentation clearly. These sections are more like halakhic midrash than narrative.
6. **Ge'ez hapaxes and difficult readings**: where the Ethiopic is uncertain, note it. Charles 1902 is 120+ years old; his English translation is a reference but not authoritative.

## Style / register

- Mix of narrative retelling (close to Genesis/Exodus but with additions and omissions) and direct legal/paraenetic address.
- The narrative sections should track the Genesis style when paralleling it.
- The legal/discourse sections have a more didactic, formal register.

## Key vocabulary

- **"Jubilee" (יובל / Ethiopic: yobel)**: the 49-year cycle. Keep "jubilee" as the technical term.
- **"The Angel of the Presence" / "the Angel of the Countenance"**: the angelic mediator — not just any angel; this is a high-ranking angel with privileged access.
- **"The Heavenly Tablets"**: the divine record from which the Angel reads. Keep "Heavenly Tablets" — do not paraphrase as "the divine record."
- **"Fornication" / sexual boundary violations**: Jubilees is especially strict on sexual ethics (chs. 25, 30, 33, 41). Render the explicit terms from the Ge'ez accurately.

## Known pitfalls

- **Harmonizing with Genesis.** Jubilees consciously reorders, adds, and omits relative to Genesis. Translate what Jubilees says, not what Genesis says.
- **Losing the legal force of the expansions.** Many narrative additions exist to justify specific halakhic rulings; that double function must be audible.
- **Approximate date calculations.** The jubilee chronology is the book's structural backbone; precision matters.
