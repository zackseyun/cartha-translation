# Acts (Acts of the Apostles) — book context for review

- **Source**: Nestle-Aland 28 / SBL Greek NT. 28 chapters.
- **Form**: Historical narrative — the second volume of Luke-Acts. Covers the early church from Pentecost to Paul's arrival in Rome.
- **Date**: ca. 80–90 AD (same author as Luke).
- **Original audience**: Theophilus and Gentile Christian readers; continuation of the narrative begun in Luke.

## Key translation issues

1. **The "we" passages** (16:10–17; 20:5–15; 21:1–18; 27:1–28:16): First-person plural — the narrator claims to be present. Render "we" exactly; do not switch to third person.
2. **Speeches and sermons**: Peter's Pentecost sermon (2:14–40), Stephen's speech (7:2–53), Paul's Areopagus address (17:22–31), Farewell to Ephesus (20:18–35). These are formal speeches with distinct rhetorical structure. Render with the formal elevated register of Hellenistic oratory.
3. **πνεῦμα / Πνεῦμα Ἅγιον** ("spirit / Holy Spirit"): The Spirit's activity is the driving force of Acts. Always "Holy Spirit" (not "Holy Ghost") — consistent with Luke.
4. **ἐκκλησία** ("church / assembly"): "Church" is the established English rendering. Do not substitute "congregation" inconsistently. Note: in 7:38, ἐκκλησία refers to Israel in the wilderness — "assembly" may be preferable in that verse.
5. **Geographical and ethnic terms**: Proper names of cities, regions, peoples must be rendered accurately. "Judea and Samaria and to the ends of the earth" (1:8) — the geographic sequence is programmatic; render exactly.
6. **μετάνοια / μετανοέω** ("repentance / to repent"): Prominent throughout Peter's and Paul's preaching. "Repentance" — not "change of mind" (too mild) or "conversion" (too broad).
7. **The name "the Way" (ἡ ὁδός)**: The early Christian community's self-designation (9:2; 19:9, 23; 22:4; 24:14, 22). Always "the Way" with capital W.

## Style / register

- Narrative is lively, episodic, and varied. Miracles, imprisonments, sea voyages, trial scenes — each has its own register.
- The sea voyage (ch. 27) is detailed technical nautical narrative — render specialized terms accurately.
- The Areopagus speech (17:22–31) is Luke's model of Hellenistic apologetics; the register is philosophically sophisticated.

## Known pitfalls

- **Flattening the speeches to plain narrative.** The formal speeches have elevated rhetoric; keep it.
- **Harmonizing Paul's conversion accounts** (9:1–9; 22:6–11; 26:12–18) — they differ in detail. Render each version as written, not harmonized.
- **Missing "the Way"** as a proper designation — lowercase "way" loses the sectarian identity marker.
