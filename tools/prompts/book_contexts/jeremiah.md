# Jeremiah — book context for review

- **Source**: Westminster Leningrad Codex (WLC) / Unicode Hebrew Bible (UHB). 52 chapters. Note: the LXX version of Jeremiah is significantly shorter and in different order than the MT.
- **Form**: Complex compilation — oracles, biographical narrative, prose sermons, laments ("confessions"), symbolic actions, and historical narrative. Not arranged chronologically.
- **Date**: Jeremiah's ministry ca. 626–586 BC (Josiah through the fall of Jerusalem and aftermath). Written/compiled largely by Jeremiah's scribe Baruch.
- **Original audience**: Judah facing and then experiencing Babylonian conquest and exile.

## Key translation issues

1. **The prose sermons vs. the poetry**: Jeremiah has three distinct registers: (a) lyric poetry (primarily chs. 1–25), (b) prose sermons in Deuteronomistic style (e.g., 7:1–8:3; 11; 18; 25), and (c) biographical/historical narrative (36–45). Render each in its appropriate register.
2. **The "confessions" / laments of Jeremiah** (11:18–12:6; 15:10–21; 17:14–18; 18:19–23; 20:7–18): Intensely personal laments to God. The language is raw and direct. Do not soften.
3. **שׁוּב** ("to return / repent / turn back"): Jeremiah's key verb for repentance — used both of Israel's unfaithfulness (they "turned away") and the call to repent ("turn back"). Render consistently to keep the wordplay audible.
4. **The "new covenant" oracle** (31:31–34): "I will make a new covenant with the house of Israel… I will put my law within them." The term בְּרִית חֲדָשָׁה (new covenant) is used here for the only time in the OT. Render exactly; do not paraphrase.
5. **"To pluck up and to tear down… to build and to plant"** (1:10): The programmatic formula for Jeremiah's calling. Keep this exact formula wherever it recurs.
6. **פֶּשַׁע / חַטָּאת / עָוֹן** ("transgression / sin / iniquity"): Jeremiah uses all three. Distinguish: עָוֹן = "iniquity" (with connotation of guilt/consequences), פֶּשַׁע = "transgression" (willful breach), חַטָּאת = "sin" (error/offense). Keep them distinct where possible.
7. **The Septuagint divergence**: The MT and LXX Jeremiah differ substantially. The COB follows the MT (WLC). Do not harmonize with the LXX; translate the MT text consistently.

## Style / register

- Poetic oracles: elevated, powerful, sometimes anguished. Preserve the rhetorical force.
- Prose sermons: repetitive, formulaic — the repetition is deliberate (Deuteronomistic rhetoric).
- Biographical narrative: straightforward prose with dramatic scenes (reading the scroll, 36; the cistern, 38).

## Known pitfalls

- **Smoothing the abruptness** of Jeremiah's laments — the rawness is authentic.
- **Importing Christian typology** into the "new covenant" passage. Translate; footnote the NT connection.
- **Confusing MT and LXX ordering.** Stay with MT throughout.
