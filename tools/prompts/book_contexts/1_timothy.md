# 1 Timothy — book context for review

- **Source**: Nestle-Aland 28 / SBL Greek NT. 6 chapters.
- **Form**: Pastoral epistle — instructions for church order, addressed to Paul's delegate Timothy. (Deutero-Pauline for most critical scholars.)
- **Date**: ca. 90–110 AD (if pseudonymous) or ca. 62–64 AD (if Pauline).
- **Original audience**: Timothy as Paul's delegate managing the church at Ephesus; addresses false teachers, church offices, community ethics.

## Key translation issues

1. **ἐπίσκοπος / πρεσβύτερος / διάκονος** ("overseer/bishop / elder / deacon"): The church office vocabulary. Render: ἐπίσκοπος = "overseer" (not "bishop," which is too ecclesiastically loaded for the early church context, though it is the etymological descendant). πρεσβύτερος = "elder." διάκονος = "deacon" (or "servant" in non-office contexts). Footnote the development of these roles.
2. **"The husband of one wife"** (3:2, 12 — μιᾶς γυναικὸς ἄνδρα): The qualification for leaders. Render literally "husband of one wife" — do not paraphrase ("faithful to his wife"), as that resolves a debated meaning.
3. **αὐθεντεῖν** (2:12 — "to exercise authority / to have authority over / to domineer"): One of the most debated words in the NT (a NT hapax). Render with a footnote — the word's meaning is genuinely contested.
4. **The "faithful saying" formulas** (πιστὸς ὁ λόγος, 1:15; 3:1; 4:9): "This is a trustworthy saying." Keep the formula exactly — it recurs in the Pastoral Epistles and must be consistent.
5. **"Sound doctrine" (ὑγιαίνουσα διδασκαλία)**: The Pastorals' characteristic term. Always "sound doctrine" — not "healthy teaching" (though ὑγιαίνω literally means "healthy").
6. **εὐσέβεια** ("godliness / piety"): A Pastoral Epistles term not prominent in Paul's undisputed letters. "Godliness" — not "religion."

## Style / register

- Practical, institutional, measured. Less argumentative than the undisputed Pauline letters.
- The "faithful sayings" are formal creedal fragments — render them with formulaic weight.

## Known pitfalls

- **Translating αὐθεντεῖν (2:12) without a footnote.** The word is genuinely uncertain.
- **Over-ecclesiasticalizing ἐπίσκοπος as "bishop."**
- **Varying the "faithful saying" formula** across its occurrences.
