# Zechariah — book context for review

- **Source**: Westminster Leningrad Codex (MT). LXX (Rahlfs) and 4QXIIa (Qumran) for text-critical reference.
- **Form**: Two major sections. Chapters 1–8 (Deutero-Zechariah): prophetic visions with angelic interpreter, dated to 520–518 BC, post-exilic. Chapters 9–14 (Trito-Zechariah / Second Zechariah): undated apocalyptic oracles, widely seen as later composition.
- **Date**: 520 BC for chs. 1–8; chs. 9–14 probably 5th–4th c. BC (or later). The two sections differ markedly in style and theology.
- **Original audience**: Post-exilic Judah rebuilding the Temple under Zerubbabel and Joshua the high priest.

## Key translation issues

1. **The Night Visions** (chs. 1–6): Eight symbolic visions interpreted by an angelic mediator (מַלְאָךְ). Preserve the vision report formula ("I lifted my eyes and saw…") and the question-and-answer dialogue with the interpreting angel. Do not explain away the apocalyptic imagery.
2. **The angel of the LORD** (מַלְאַךְ יהוה): distinct from the interpreting angel; appears as divine agent in 1:11–17; 3:1–10. Keep the distinction clear.
3. **צֶמַח** (3:8; 6:12 — "the Branch"): messianic title (cf. Isaiah 4:2; Jeremiah 23:5; 33:15). Always render "the Branch" — this is a technical messianic term, not merely a plant metaphor.
4. **Joshua and Zerubbabel** (chs. 3–4; 6): the two anointed ones (4:14). The "two olive trees" / "two anointed ones" imagery must be preserved as-is; do not collapse them.
5. **Chapters 9–14**: The apocalyptic oracles include the entry of the king on a donkey (9:9 — cited in Matthew 21:5), the thirty pieces of silver (11:12–13 — cited in Matthew 27:9–10), and the pierced one (12:10 — cited in John 19:37). Render each from the Hebrew; note the NT echoes in footnotes, not in the translation itself.
6. **"Living waters"** (14:8): eschatological imagery. Preserve literally.

## Style / register

- Chs. 1–8: Visionary prose with formal report language.
- Chs. 9–14: Elevated apocalyptic poetry with dense imagery. Distinct from chs. 1–8; the register is more intense and less structured.
- Do not blend the two sections' styles.

## Known pitfalls

- **Harmonizing Zechariah's messianic texts to their NT applications.** Render from the Hebrew; the NT authors are applying texts with their own hermeneutic.
- **Losing the formal structure of the vision reports.** The interpretation dialogues in chs. 1–6 are tightly structured; keep that structure.
- **Translating 12:10 "when they look on me, the one they have pierced"** without noting the textual issue (MT: "me"; LXX / Syriac: "him"). Footnote the variant.
