# Susanna — book context for review

- **Source**: LXX (Rahlfs — Theodotion version preferred as more widely attested). One chapter (64 verses). An addition to Daniel (placed before Daniel 1 in Theodotion, after ch. 12 in the Old Greek/LXX).
- **Form**: Short novella. A beautiful and righteous woman, Susanna, is falsely accused of adultery by two lecherous elders. She is condemned, but Daniel exposes the elders' lie through cross-examination, and they are executed instead.
- **Date**: 2nd–1st c. BC.
- **Original audience**: Jewish readers; a wisdom tale with legal and moral themes (the danger of false witness, the importance of cross-examination, divine vindication of the innocent).

## Key translation issues

1. **The false accusation**: Two elders accuse Susanna of adultery under an oak/mastic tree (the specific trees matter in the story because Daniel's cross-examination exploits the different tree names, linking each elder's lie to the punishment — "under a mastic tree → cut in two [σχίνου/σχίσει]"). This wordplay works in Greek; the translation should note it.
2. **The cross-examination**: Daniel's interrogation technique (separating the witnesses and asking different questions) was celebrated as proto-forensic procedure. Render the procedural logic clearly.
3. **ψευδομαρτύρια** ("false testimony"): a technical legal term. "False testimony" or "false witness" — not just "lies."
4. **Susanna's prayer** (vv. 42–43): short but sincere prayer of the falsely accused. Elevated register.
5. **The tree wordplay** is not renderable in English; a footnote explaining it is essential when translating the verses with the cross-examination.

## Style / register

- Terse narrative prose; the novella style is efficient and dramatic.
- The cross-examination scene should read as legally focused and deliberate.
- Susanna's virtue is presented matter-of-factly; do not over-sentimentalize.

## Known pitfalls

- **Missing the tree wordplay** without a footnote. The story's resolution depends on this.
- **Under-rendering the procedural logic of the cross-examination.** The forensic precision is the point of Daniel's intervention.
