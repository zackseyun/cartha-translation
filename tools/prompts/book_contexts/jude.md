# Jude — book context for review

- **Source**: Nestle-Aland 28 / SBL Greek NT. 1 chapter (25 verses).
- **Form**: Brief epistle — polemical, citing both canonical OT and extra-canonical texts (1 Enoch, Assumption of Moses). Attacks false teachers.
- **Date**: ca. 65–90 AD.
- **Original audience**: Undefined; addressed "to those who are called, beloved in God the Father and kept for Jesus Christ."

## Key translation issues

1. **Extra-canonical citations**: Jude cites 1 Enoch 1:9 (v. 14–15) explicitly and alludes to the Assumption of Moses (v. 9). Render these citations faithfully as Jude gives them — do not import the wording from the 1 Enoch translation in the COB corpus.
2. **ἀγωνίζεσθαι** (v. 3 — "contend / struggle"): "I found it necessary to write appealing to you to contend (ἐπαγωνίζεσθαι) for the faith." Keep "contend" — the athletic/military imagery is strong.
3. **ἅπαξ παραδοθείσῃ** (v. 3 — "once for all delivered"): "The faith that was once for all delivered to the saints." "Once for all" — not "once" alone.
4. **σπιλάδες** (v. 12 — "hidden reefs / blemishes / love-feasts"): The word can mean "reefs" (dangerous rocks) or "spots/blemishes." Most modern translations read "hidden reefs at your love feasts." Footnote the textual ambiguity.
5. **The doxology** (vv. 24–25): "To him who is able to keep you from stumbling and to present you blameless before the presence of his glory with great joy, to the only God, our Savior, through Jesus Christ our Lord, be glory, majesty, dominion, and authority, before all time and now and forever. Amen." One of the NT's great doxologies. Render in full liturgical register.
6. **ἀγάπαις** (v. 12 — "love feasts"): The term for Christian communal meals. Keep "love feasts."

## Style / register

- Dense, compressed, rhetorically intense — 25 verses packing enormous polemical energy.
- The closing doxology (vv. 24–25) is elevated liturgical prose — preserve the sweep.

## Known pitfalls

- **Missing the closing doxology's full weight** — it is one of the finest in the NT.
- **Harmonizing Jude's wording with 2 Peter's parallel** — render Jude's own text.
