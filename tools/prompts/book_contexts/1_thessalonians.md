# 1 Thessalonians — book context for review

- **Source**: Nestle-Aland 28 / SBL Greek NT. 5 chapters.
- **Form**: Pauline letter — likely Paul's earliest surviving letter. Warm, pastoral, eschatologically focused.
- **Date**: ca. 49–51 AD from Corinth. Possibly the earliest NT document.
- **Original audience**: The young church at Thessalonica, anxious about fellow believers who have died before Christ's return.

## Key translation issues

1. **παρουσία** (2:19; 3:13; 4:15; 5:23 — "coming / arrival / presence"): Paul's term for Christ's return. "Coming" is the traditional rendering. Keep "coming" — not "arrival" or "presence" (which loses the eschatological technical use).
2. **The rapture passage** (4:13–18): "We who are alive… will be caught up together with them in the clouds to meet the Lord in the air." The vocabulary is precisely apocalyptic — ἀπάντησις (formal civic welcome/meeting), ἁρπαγησόμεθα (will be caught up). Render literally.
3. **νήπιος / τροφός** (2:7 — "infant / nursing mother"): Textual variant — the NA28 reads νήπιοι (we were like infants) vs. ἤπιοι (gentle). The NA28 reads νήπιοι; render accordingly with a footnote.
4. **ἁγιασμός** (4:3–8 — "sanctification / holiness"): "This is the will of God, your sanctification (ἁγιασμός)." Keep "sanctification" — not "moral purity" (too narrow).
5. **"Pray without ceasing"** (5:17 — ἀδιαλείπτως προσεύχεσθε): "Pray without ceasing." Keep the traditional rendering — not "pray continually" (which slightly weakens the intensity).
6. **The "Day of the Lord"** (5:2): "Like a thief in the night." Keep "Day of the Lord" (ἡμέρα κυρίου) as a proper eschatological term.

## Style / register

- Warm and affectionate. Paul's thanksgiving sections are genuine and personal.
- Eschatological sections (4:13–5:11) are urgent and precise.
- Short, direct ethical instructions (5:12–22): "Rejoice always, pray without ceasing, give thanks in all circumstances."

## Known pitfalls

- **Varying παρουσία** — keep consistent across all four occurrences.
- **Getting the νήπιος/ἤπιος textual variant right** — NA28 reads νήπιοι; note in a footnote.
