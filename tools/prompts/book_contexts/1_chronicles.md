# 1 Chronicles — book context for review

- **Source**: Westminster Leningrad Codex (WLC) / Unicode Hebrew Bible (UHB). 29 chapters.
- **Form**: Narrative and genealogy. Opens with extensive genealogies (1–9), then narrates Saul's death and David's reign with focus on cult and temple preparation.
- **Date**: Post-exilic, ca. 400–350 BC (Chronicler's history).
- **Original audience**: Post-exilic Jewish community — establishing continuity with pre-exilic identity, with special focus on temple worship, Levites, and Davidic covenant.

## Key translation issues

1. **Genealogical lists** (chs. 1–9): The genealogies must be rendered accurately with consistent proper name transliteration. The Hebrew form of names may differ from their Genesis counterparts — render as Chronicles gives them, footnoting significant variant forms.
2. **The Chronicler's omissions**: 1 Chronicles omits David's sins (Bathsheba, Uriah) and Saul's legitimate career. Do not import them from Samuel.
3. **Levitical roles and temple organization** (15–16; 23–29): Precise vocabulary for musical guilds (singers, gatekeepers, Levitical divisions). Keep all names and roles consistently.
4. **David's preparations for the temple**: The Chronicler's key contribution — David cannot build the temple because he is a "man of war" (22:8), but he prepares everything. "A man of wars" — keep the phrase.
5. **"The hand of God"** and **"the spirit of God"**: The Chronicler's characteristic narrative explanations. Keep these theological glosses.
6. **Prayer of David** (29:10–19): A formal doxological prayer of great dignity. Render with full elevation.

## Style / register

- Formal, cultic, systematizing. Less narrative texture than Samuel/Kings.
- Genealogies are deliberately dry lists — maintain accuracy, not elegance.
- David's speeches and prayers have elevated register.

## Known pitfalls

- **Harmonizing with Samuel** by importing material the Chronicler omitted.
- **Inconsistent proper name spellings** between the genealogies and the narrative sections.
