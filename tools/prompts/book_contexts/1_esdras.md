# 1 Esdras — book context for review

- **Source**: LXX (Rahlfs / Hanhart). Also called "Greek Ezra" or "Esdras A." 9 chapters. Overlaps with Chronicles, Ezra, and Nehemiah.
- **Form**: Historical narrative covering the same events as 2 Chronicles 35–36, Ezra, and Nehemiah 7:38–8:12, with additions and variations. Includes the famous "Three Bodyguards" episode (3:1–5:6) which is unique to this book.
- **Date**: 2nd–1st c. BC. The relationship to Ezra-Nehemiah is complex; this may represent an earlier version of the Ezra tradition.
- **Original audience**: Greek-speaking Jews; possibly Alexandrian.

## Key translation issues

1. **Relationship to canonical Ezra-Nehemiah**: 1 Esdras covers similar material but with different wording and arrangement. Translate from 1 Esdras directly — do not harmonize with Ezra-Nehemiah.
2. **The "Three Bodyguards" debate** (3:1–5:6): Three soldiers debate what is the strongest thing in the world (wine, the king, women, truth). Zerubbabel wins by arguing for Truth (ἀλήθεια). This unique section has a philosophical-rhetorical register; render the arguments with precision.
3. **Zerubbabel's speech** (4:34–41): elevated philosophical-rhetorical address. The climactic line "Great is Truth, and it prevails above all" is a famous aphorism — keep its aphoristic force.
4. **Temple restoration narrative**: the cultic restoration language echoes Chronicles and Ezra. Keep the liturgical vocabulary (Levites, courses, first-fruits, etc.) consistent with those books.
5. **Official correspondence** (2:15–26; 6:8–22): Persian royal letters; official register required.

## Style / register

- Historical narrative for most of the book.
- The bodyguards debate is a philosophical-rhetorical showpiece; elevated register.
- Cultic and administrative sections have a more formal, institutional tone.

## Known pitfalls

- **Harmonizing to Ezra-Nehemiah** where 1 Esdras differs. Translate 1 Esdras as it stands.
- **Under-rendering Zerubbabel's rhetorical speech.** It is a formal competition; the eloquence matters.
