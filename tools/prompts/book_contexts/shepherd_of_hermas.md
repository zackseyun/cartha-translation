# Shepherd of Hermas — book context for review and drafting

- **Work**: Shepherd of Hermas
- **Likely date**: 2nd century AD
- **Likely setting**: Roman Christianity
- **Primary witness for this workflow**: Greek (Lightfoot 1891 OCR / normalized source layer)
- **Major divisions**: Visions, Mandates, Similitudes
- **Probable genre mix**: apocalypse, prophetic exhortation, catechetical moral instruction, symbolic allegory

## What matters most for translation

1. **Hermas shifts register often.** The Visions are visionary and symbolic, the Mandates are paraenetic and direct, and the Similitudes are allegorical and often extended.
2. **Do not flatten the shepherd's moral instruction into generic devotional prose.** It often speaks with force, repetition, and concrete ethical imagery.
3. **Preserve the second-century church texture.** Terms about repentance, purity, simplicity, double-mindedness, the holy spirit, fasting, and ecclesial life should remain concrete and early-Christian rather than modernized into vague spirituality.
4. **Keep the allegories legible.** The tower, shepherd, rods, willow, angels, stones, and vines are not decorative—they carry the argument.
5. **Do not over-harmonize with the New Testament.** Hermas may echo familiar Christian language, but it should still sound like Hermas.

## Style / register

- Aim for elevated but readable English.
- Keep repeated moral exhortations repeated.
- Preserve rhetorical questions and interpretive explanations.
- Prefer transparent translation over smoothing away strange or dense symbolic logic.
