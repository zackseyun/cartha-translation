# Philemon — book context for review

- **Source**: Nestle-Aland 28 / SBL Greek NT. 1 chapter (25 verses). The shortest Pauline letter.
- **Form**: Personal letter from Paul to Philemon regarding the runaway slave Onesimus.
- **Date**: ca. 60 AD (during Paul's imprisonment, either Rome or Caesarea).
- **Original audience**: Philemon — a housechurch leader; the letter asks him to receive Onesimus back as a brother.

## Key translation issues

1. **δοῦλος** (v. 16 — "slave"): Onesimus is described as no longer a slave but "a beloved brother." The word is δοῦλος — "slave," not "servant." The social reality must not be softened.
2. **ὀνήσιμος** ("useful/profitable"): The name Onesimus means "useful." Paul makes a wordplay in v. 11: "Formerly he was useless (ἄχρηστον) to you, but now he is indeed useful (εὔχρηστον) to you and to me." The pun on the name should be noted in a footnote.
3. **χρηστός / Χριστός** (the name/useful wordplay): Related to the above; "useful" (χρήσιμος) and "Christ" (Χριστός) were near-homophones in Greek. Footnote.
4. **παρακαλέω** (v. 9 — "appeal / urge / entreat"): Paul "appeals" rather than commands — "I appeal to you on the basis of love." The verb choice is deliberate diplomacy. Keep "appeal."
5. **The social/ethical ambiguity**: Paul neither condemns slavery explicitly nor demands Onesimus's freedom outright. Render the text as it is — do not resolve the ambiguity either toward liberation theology or toward endorsing slavery. Translate; let footnotes handle the ethics.
6. **"Receive him as you would receive me"** (v. 17): The identification of Paul with Onesimus is rhetorically striking — preserve the directness.

## Style / register

- Rhetorically sophisticated for a brief letter. Paul uses politeness conventions and subtle social pressure.
- Warm but carefully calculated — the warmth is diplomatic.

## Known pitfalls

- **Rendering δοῦλος as "servant"** — Onesimus is a slave; the social reality matters.
- **Resolving the ethical ambiguity** in the translation text — translate and footnote.
