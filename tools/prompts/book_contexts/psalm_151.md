# Psalm 151 — book context for review

- **Source**: LXX (Rahlfs). Also attested at Qumran (11QPsalms^a, in Hebrew — and as two separate psalms there). One chapter (7 verses in LXX).
- **Form**: A psalm attributed to David "outside the number" — the LXX superscription explicitly says this was composed by David "when he killed Goliath." A brief autobiographical poem.
- **Date**: Composition uncertain; the Qumran Hebrew version may be earlier than the LXX.
- **Original audience**: Greek-speaking Jews who knew this additional psalm in the Greek psalter.

## Key translation issues

1. **The superscription**: "This psalm is genuinely Davidic, though outside the number; about the single combat of David with Goliath." The phrase "outside the number" (ἔξω τοῦ ἀριθμοῦ) is significant — keep it literally; this is the LXX's explicit acknowledgment that the psalm is supernumerary.
2. **Autobiographical voice** (vv. 1–2): David speaks about his smallness among his brothers, his shepherding work, his artistic gifts. Render in first-person poetic voice.
3. **Goliath episode** (vv. 6–7): rapid and precise. Keep the terse, confident narrative.
4. **Note the relationship to 1 Samuel 16–17**: Psalm 151 summarizes David's anointing and the Goliath episode. Do not harmonize vocabulary with 1 Samuel; render from the psalm text.

## Style / register

- Short lyric poem with autobiographical narrative. Elevated but not complex.
- The brevity means every word carries weight.
