# 3 John — book context for review

- **Source**: Nestle-Aland 28 / SBL Greek NT. 1 chapter (15 verses). The shortest NT book by word count.
- **Form**: Personal letter from "the Elder" to Gaius. A private note about hospitality, church authority, and a specific conflict involving Diotrephes.
- **Date**: ca. 90–110 AD. Same Johannine community as 1–2 John and the Gospel of John.
- **Original audience**: Gaius — a specific individual commended for his hospitality to traveling missionaries.

## Key translation issues

1. **ὁ πρεσβύτερος** ("the Elder"): The author's self-designation. Keep "the elder" — not "the presbyter" (too technical) or "the old man" (too colloquial).
2. **ψυχή** (v. 2 — "soul"): "I pray that all may go well with you and that you may be in good health, as it goes well with your soul." Keep "soul" — not "as you are doing so well spiritually."
3. **Gaius, Diotrephes, Demetrius**: Three named individuals. Keep all three names transliterated consistently.
4. **φιλοπρωτεύων** (v. 9 — "loves to be first / likes to put himself first"): The description of Diotrephes. Keep "loves to be first" — the compound communicates ambition for preeminence.
5. **ἀλήθεια** ("truth"): As in the rest of the Johannine corpus, "truth" is a theological term — "walking in the truth" (v. 3, 4). Keep "truth" with its full Johannine weight.
6. **The parallel structure with 2 John**: 3 John uses similar opening and closing forms as 2 John. Render consistently with 2 John's vocabulary.

## Style / register

- Brief, personal, direct. Unlike the theological density of 1 John.
- The conflict with Diotrephes is described with measured restraint — preserve the tone.

## Known pitfalls

- **Over-theologizing a simple personal letter.** The letter is primarily pastoral and practical.
- **Inconsistent rendering with 2 John** — same author, same letter formula; vocabulary should match.
