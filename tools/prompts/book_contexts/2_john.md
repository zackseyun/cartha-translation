# 2 John — book context for review

- **Source**: Nestle-Aland 28 / SBL Greek NT. Single chapter (13 verses).
- **Form**: Genuine letter format — greeting, body, closing. The shortest NT book.
- **Author / recipient**: "The Elder" writing to "the chosen lady and her children" — likely a metaphor for a house church community.
- **Date**: Late 1st c. AD. Related to the Johannine epistolary tradition (shares vocabulary and concerns with 1 John).
- **Original audience**: A local Christian community threatened by traveling teachers who deny the incarnation ("do not confess that Jesus Christ has come in the flesh").

## Key translation issues

1. **ἀλήθεια** ("truth"): functions as a near-technical term in Johannine literature — "the truth" = the authentic gospel / the reality revealed in Christ. Render consistently as "the truth," not "what is true" or "truth" without the article where the Greek has the article.
2. **ἀγάπη** ("love"): v. 5–6 — "love one another" / "this is love, that we walk according to his commandments." The love-commandment connection. Keep "love" (not "charity").
3. **πλάνος / πλανάω** (v. 7 — "deceiver / to deceive"): the false teachers are πλάνοι, "deceivers." Render plainly; do not soften.
4. **ἀντίχριστος** (v. 7 — "antichrist"): keep "antichrist" — it is a technical Johannine term.
5. **μισθόν** (v. 8 — "reward / wages"): preserve the specific vocabulary — "full reward," not "full reward for your work."
6. **χαίρειν** (v. 10–11 — "to greet"): the instruction not to "receive into the house" or "give a greeting" to false teachers. "Greeting" is the idiom; keep it.

## Style / register

- Personal, warm but alert. The letter's affectional opening ("whom I love in truth") and closing ("face to face") frame a serious doctrinal warning.
- The brevity means every word matters; do not expand with paraphrase.

## Known pitfalls

- **Rendering ἀλήθεια as generic "truth."** In Johannine literature it has a specific technical charge; the article is important.
- **Softening the warning against the deceivers.** The letter is quite direct; the translation should be too.
