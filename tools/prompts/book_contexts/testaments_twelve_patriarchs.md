# Testaments of the Twelve Patriarchs — book context for drafting

- **Work**: Testaments of the Twelve Patriarchs
- **Form**: twelve farewell/testament speeches placed on the lips of Jacob's sons
- **Language of this drafting layer**: Greek witness text
- **Likely literary character**: Jewish ethical / paraenetic testamentary literature with apocalyptic and prophetic expansions in some sections
- **Likely setting of composition**: Second Temple Jewish tradition, with complex transmission and later Christian interpolation in some passages

## What matters most for translation

1. **Each testament has its own voice.** Reuben, Simeon, Levi, Judah, Joseph, etc. should not all sound identical.
2. **These are exhortation-heavy texts.** Preserve the father-to-sons tone, moral urgency, and directness.
3. **Do not flatten the texture into generic devotional prose.** These texts mix confession, warning, praise, prophecy, and testamentary command.
4. **Be transparent around likely Christian interpolation or textual expansion.** If a passage feels secondary, translate what is in the Greek witness honestly, and use notes rather than silently harmonizing it.
5. **Keep repeated vice/virtue vocabulary stable within each testament.** Many chapters revolve around a thematic thread such as envy, lust, wrath, simplicity, mercy, or purity of mind.

## Style / register

- Elevated but readable English.
- Strong imperatives should stay strong.
- Preserve rhetorical parallelism and repeated moral formulas where possible.
- Do not over-modernize kinship language, blessing language, vice lists, or prophetic language.
