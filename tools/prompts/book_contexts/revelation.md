# Revelation (Apocalypse) — book context for review

- **Source**: Nestle-Aland 28 / SBL Greek NT. 22 chapters.
- **Form**: Christian apocalypse with epistolary frame (letters to seven churches, chs. 2–3). Visionary narrative, heavenly scenes, symbolic numerical sequences (seals, trumpets, bowls), eschatological climax.
- **Date**: ca. 95 AD under Domitian (consensus), though earlier dates under Nero (ca. 68 AD) are argued.
- **Original audience**: Seven churches in Asia Minor under Roman imperial pressure.

## Key translation issues

1. **Apocalyptic symbolic vocabulary**: the symbols are not puzzles to be decoded in the translation but images to be rendered. Do not explain; translate. What the "beast," "Babylon," "number 666," "the Lamb" mean theologically belongs in footnotes, not the main text.
2. **ἀρνίον** ("the Lamb"): the distinctive Johannine/Revelation word for Christ. Always "the Lamb" — not "the lamb" (lowercase) when used as a divine title.
3. **ἄξιος** ("worthy"): the throne-room acclamation ("Worthy is the Lamb…" 5:9, 12). Keep "worthy" — this is a formal acclamation term.
4. **ἐγώ εἰμι τὸ Ἄλφα καὶ τὸ Ὦ** (1:8; 22:13 — "I am the Alpha and the Omega"): keep literally. Also "the first and the last," "the beginning and the end" — these are three related but distinct divine self-descriptions; render each one in its specific occurrence.
5. **Liturgical formulas**: "Holy, holy, holy is the Lord God Almighty" (4:8) — "Almighty" for παντοκράτωρ. The threefold "holy" must be kept as three.
6. **Numbers**: the symbolic use of 7, 12, 1000, 144,000, 666, 3.5 years ("a time, times, and half a time" / 1260 days / 42 months). Render exactly as written; do not normalize.
7. **"Babylon"** (chs. 17–18): render as "Babylon" — the symbolic name for Rome. Do not substitute "Rome" in the text.
8. **The Greek of Revelation**: famously rough and Semitically influenced. Do not over-polish it into smooth literary Greek — some of the awkwardness is original.

## Style / register

- Visionary, symbolic, intensely heightened. The register is sublime throughout.
- Preserve the repetitive liturgical formulas ("and I saw," "and I heard") — they have structural function.
- The hymns (4:8, 11; 5:9–13; 7:10–12; etc.) should be rendered as poetry with their full liturgical weight.
- The oracles to the seven churches (chs. 2–3) shift to a more direct, pastoral address.

## Known pitfalls

- **Over-interpreting symbols in the translation.** Render the image; footnote the interpretation.
- **Smoothing the Greek roughness** of Revelation into polished modern prose.
- **Rendering the numbers symbolically** (e.g., paraphrasing 144,000 as "the full number of the redeemed"). Translate the number; footnote the interpretation.
