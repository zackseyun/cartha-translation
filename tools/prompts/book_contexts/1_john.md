# 1 John — book context for review

- **Source**: Nestle-Aland 28 / SBL Greek NT. 5 chapters.
- **Form**: Epistle (though more of a homily — no opening greeting). Addresses docetic schismatics who have left the community.
- **Date**: ca. 90–110 AD. Same Johannine community as the Gospel of John.
- **Original audience**: A Johannine community after a schism — "they went out from us" (2:19).

## Key translation issues

1. **ἐν τῷ φωτί / ἐν τῇ σκοτίᾳ** ("in the light / in the darkness"): The light/darkness dualism is structural. Keep the prepositional phrase "in the light" / "in the darkness" — do not vary.
2. **ἀγάπη** ("love"): "God is love" (4:8, 16). The love vocabulary is pervasive and theologically weighted. Render consistently — never swap in "affection" or "care."
3. **ἀντίχριστος** (2:18, 22; 4:3 — "antichrist"): Keep "antichrist" — the word appears here for the first time in the Bible. Do not substitute "opponent of Christ."
4. **τέκνα θεοῦ / τέκνα τοῦ διαβόλου** ("children of God / children of the devil"): The binary division of humanity in 3:1–10. Keep "children" — not "sons" (the Greek is τέκνα, not υἱοί).
5. **The "tests"**: 1 John offers repeated tests for genuine faith — "By this we know…" (ἐν τούτῳ γινώσκομεν). Keep the formula consistent.
6. **ἱλασμός** (2:2; 4:10 — "propitiation / expiation / atoning sacrifice"): Same debate as Romans 3:25 (ἱλαστήριον). Render consistently within 1 John; footnote the options.
7. **The Johannine Comma** (5:7 — "the Father, the Word, and the Holy Spirit"): The Trinitarian gloss present in some Latin MSS and late Greek MSS is not in the earliest Greek manuscripts and is not in the NA28. The COB follows NA28 — this clause should NOT appear in the main text (may appear in a text-critical footnote if the project includes those).

## Style / register

- Simple, repetitive, spiraling Johannine style — the same ideas return in new configurations.
- The love section (4:7–21) is the emotional and theological center; keep the lyric repetition.
- More polemical in places than the Gospel (the schism is close and painful).

## Known pitfalls

- **Including the Johannine Comma in the main text** — it is not in the critical text.
- **Losing the light/darkness binary** by varying the prepositions.
- **Rendering "children" as "sons"** — John uses τέκνα, not υἱοί, in this epistle.
