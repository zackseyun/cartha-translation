# 2 Peter — book context for review

- **Source**: Nestle-Aland 28 / SBL Greek NT. 3 chapters.
- **Form**: Epistle — testamentary letter attributed to Peter. Addresses false teachers and the delay of the Parousia.
- **Date**: ca. 100–125 AD. The latest NT letter, likely pseudonymous.
- **Original audience**: Christians troubled by false teachers who deny the Parousia and use freedom as license for immorality.

## Key translation issues

1. **The elaborate Greek style**: 2 Peter has the most Hellenistic and rhetorically ornate Greek in the NT. Preserve the elevated diction — do not simplify.
2. **θεία φύσις** (1:4 — "divine nature"): "You may become partakers of the divine nature." A rare phrase in the NT — render literally. "Participants in the divine nature" is accurate.
3. **The virtue list** (1:5–7): "Add to your faith virtue, and to virtue knowledge, and to knowledge self-control…" The chain of seven virtues must be rendered as a precise list, each term distinct.
4. **"Eyewitnesses of his majesty"** (1:16 — ἐπόπται γενηθέντες τῆς ἐκείνου μεγαλειότητος): Keep "eyewitnesses" and "majesty."
5. **The Jude parallel**: Much of chapters 2–3 parallels Jude. Render 2 Peter on its own terms — do not harmonize to Jude's wording.
6. **"A thousand years is as one day"** (3:8): "With the Lord one day is as a thousand years, and a thousand years as one day." The full chiasm must be kept — not shortened.
7. **"The elements will melt"** (3:10, 12 — στοιχεῖα): The dissolution of the cosmos. "Elements" — whether physical elements or cosmic powers is debated; footnote.

## Style / register

- Rhetorically elevated, dense, sometimes labored.
- The eschatological urgency of chapter 3 is heightened — preserve the intensity.

## Known pitfalls

- **Harmonizing with Jude** — translate 2 Peter's own wording.
- **Shortening the "thousand years / one day" chiasm** (3:8).
