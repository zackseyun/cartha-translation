# Titus — book context for review

- **Source**: Nestle-Aland 28 / SBL Greek NT. Three chapters.
- **Form**: Pauline pastoral letter to Titus, a delegate on Crete. Part of the Pastoral Epistles (1 Tim, 2 Tim, Titus). Concerns church order, elder qualifications, and doctrinal integrity.
- **Date**: Disputed. If Pauline: ca. 60s AD. If pseudepigraphical: late 1st / early 2nd c. AD. Either way, the theology and vocabulary are consistent with the Pastoral Epistles corpus.
- **Original audience**: Titus, and by extension church leaders in the Cretan churches he is overseeing.

## Key translation issues

1. **σωτήρ** ("Savior"): appears with emphasis throughout (1:3, 4; 2:10, 13; 3:4, 6). Both God and Christ are called σωτήρ in close proximity. Render consistently as "Savior" and do not collapse the distinction between "God our Savior" and "our great God and Savior Jesus Christ."
2. **Titus 2:13** — "our great God and Savior Jesus Christ": one of the clearest grammatical arguments for the divinity of Christ in the NT (Granville Sharp rule). The Greek allows "the great God and Savior of us, Jesus Christ" as a reference to one person. Do not render in a way that separates God and Jesus Christ into two subjects. Footnote the grammatical discussion.
3. **ἐπιφάνεια** ("appearing / epiphany"): technical term for Christ's coming (1:3; 2:11, 13; 3:4). Render as "appearing" or "manifestation" — not the weaker "coming" or "arrival."
4. **σώφρων / σωφροσύνη** ("self-controlled / sound-minded / sensible"): a cluster of related virtue terms throughout the letter (1:8; 2:2, 4, 5, 6, 12). Render consistently — "self-controlled" or "sound-minded"; do not use three different English words for what is a single repeated Greek virtue.
5. **καλὰ ἔργα** ("good works / good deeds"): the Pastoral Epistles' practical ethical emphasis. Render consistently as "good works" or "good deeds."
6. **The Cretan quotation** (1:12 — "Cretans are always liars, evil beasts, lazy gluttons"): Paul quotes a Cretan poet (likely Epimenides). Keep the quotation's bluntness; do not soften it.

## Style / register

- Practical, direct, organizational. Less rhetorical than the main Pauline letters.
- The virtue lists and household code material (2:1–10) have a catechetical register.
- The doctrinal summaries (2:11–14; 3:3–7) are compact theological prose; render their precision.

## Known pitfalls

- **Blurring the "God our Savior" / "Christ our Savior" distinction throughout the letter.**
- **Under-translating σώφρων** — "sensible" loses the specific Greco-Roman moral philosophy freight the word carries.
- **Translating 2:13 ambiguously** in a way that avoids the Granville Sharp construction. Render with a footnote.
