# Additions to Esther — book context for review

- **Source**: LXX (Rahlfs / Hanhart). The Greek Esther contains six major additions (A–F) not found in the Hebrew MT. Total: about 107 additional verses.
- **Form**: Extended novelistic narrative. The additions supply prayers (Addition C), letters (Additions B and E), and interpretive narrative (Additions A, D, F) that the Hebrew Esther deliberately omitted.
- **Date**: Additions added ca. 2nd–1st c. BC, possibly in stages.
- **Original audience**: Greek-speaking Jews. The additions make explicit what the Hebrew deliberately left implicit: God's name and activity, the theological significance of events, the emotional experience of Esther and Mordecai.

## Key translation issues

1. **The Hebrew Esther never mentions God.** The Greek Additions explicitly supply divine theology throughout. The reviewer should note when a verse is from a Greek addition vs. the MT-equivalent portion of the book.
2. **Mordecai's Dream** (Addition A) and its interpretation (Addition F): apocalyptic dream-vision followed by its fulfillment. Render with the elevated visionary register.
3. **Mordecai's Prayer** (Addition C, 13:9–17): formal penitential prayer with explicit theological justification for not bowing to Haman. Render with liturgical register.
4. **Esther's Prayer** (Addition C, 14:1–19): Esther's private prayer, including her expression of feeling compelled to act against her will. This is a key window into her inner life absent from the Hebrew; render its emotional authenticity.
5. **The royal decrees** (Additions B and E): formal royal letters with official language. These are forgeries (literary constructs); render with official Hellenistic administrative register.
6. **Esther's approach to the king** (Addition D): elaborates MT 5:1 with psychological and dramatic detail. Render with narrative intimacy.

## Style / register

- Narrative sections: novelistic, emotionally engaged.
- Prayer sections: elevated liturgical.
- Official letters: formal administrative.

## Known pitfalls

- **Confusing which sections are the additions and which track the Hebrew MT.** The additions are clearly marked in the LXX arrangement (Alpha-F letters); the translator should track this.
- **Under-rendering the prayers.** The prayers are the theological heart of the additions; they deserve elevated rendering.
