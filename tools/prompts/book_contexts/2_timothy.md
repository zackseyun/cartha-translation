# 2 Timothy — book context for review

- **Source**: Nestle-Aland 28 / SBL Greek NT. 4 chapters.
- **Form**: Pastoral epistle — Paul's most personal letter to Timothy, written as if from prison facing execution. A farewell letter.
- **Date**: ca. 90–110 AD (if pseudonymous) or ca. 64–65 AD (if Pauline).
- **Original audience**: Timothy — encouragement to remain faithful and guard the deposit of faith.

## Key translation issues

1. **θεόπνευστος** (3:16 — "God-breathed / inspired by God"): "All Scripture is God-breathed." The only NT occurrence of this word. "God-breathed" (NIV, ESV) is the most literal. "Inspired" (NRSV) is traditional but loses the breath/Spirit imagery. Keep "God-breathed" or "inspired by God."
2. **παρακαταθήκη** ("the deposit / the trust / what has been entrusted"): "Guard the good deposit (παρακαταθήκη) entrusted to you" (1:12, 14). Keep "deposit" — it is a legal term for a trust held in safe-keeping.
3. **"I have finished the race"** (4:7): "I have fought the good fight, I have finished the race, I have kept the faith." The three-part confession uses three perfect-tense verbs. Keep the perfects: "I have fought… I have finished… I have kept."
4. **ὁ καιρὸς τῆς ἀναλύσεώς μου** (4:6 — "the time of my departure"): "My departure is at hand." ἀνάλυσις means to weigh anchor or break camp — "departure" is accurate; "death" is interpretive.
5. **The "sound doctrine" and "faithful saying" vocabulary**: Same as 1 Timothy — keep consistent.
6. **Personal names at the end** (4:9–22): The personal notes must be rendered accurately — Demas, Crescens, Titus, Luke, Mark, Carpus, Alexander, Eubulus, Pudens, Linus, Claudia. All proper names.

## Style / register

- Personal, warm, and tinged with pathos. The most emotionally affecting of the Pastorals.
- The farewell tone (4:6–8) is elevated — Paul's final self-assessment.

## Known pitfalls

- **Rendering θεόπνευστος as merely "inspired"** — the breath/Spirit imagery matters.
- **Losing the three-part rhythmic confession** of 4:7 by breaking its parallel structure.
