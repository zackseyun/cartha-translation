# 2 Chronicles — book context for review

- **Source**: Westminster Leningrad Codex (WLC) / Unicode Hebrew Bible (UHB). 36 chapters.
- **Form**: Narrative — continuation of 1 Chronicles. Solomon's reign and temple dedication (1–9), then the kings of Judah only (10–36) ending with the edict of Cyrus.
- **Date**: Post-exilic, ca. 400–350 BC (Chronicler's history).
- **Original audience**: Post-exilic Jewish community — the Davidic covenant, temple worship, and faithfulness as the keys to national life.

## Key translation issues

1. **The Chronicler's interpretation**: The Chronicler consistently explains events theologically — "he sought the LORD" / "he did not seek the LORD." Keep these narrative theological evaluations.
2. **Solomon's temple dedication prayer** (6:1–42): Closely parallel to 1 Kings 8. The two versions have small differences — render 2 Chronicles as given, not harmonized to 1 Kings.
3. **"If my people… humble themselves and pray"** (7:14): One of the most quoted OT verses. "If my people who are called by my name humble themselves, and pray and seek my face and turn from their wicked ways, then I will hear from heaven and will forgive their sin and heal their land." Keep every element of the conditional sequence.
4. **The Chronicler's unique material** on various kings: Manasseh's prayer (33:12–13, 18–19; expanded in the apocryphal Prayer of Manasseh). Do not import the Prayer of Manasseh into the 2 Chronicles text.
5. **The regnal formulas**: Different from Kings — the Chronicler uses "sought/did not seek the LORD" more than the Kings formula. Keep the Chronicler's own evaluative language.
6. **The edict of Cyrus** (36:22–23): The closing verses also open Ezra — the repetition is deliberate. Render identically in both books.

## Style / register

- Same as 1 Chronicles — formal, theologically explanatory, with elevated prayers and speeches.
- Less narrative texture than Kings; more focus on cult and faithfulness.

## Known pitfalls

- **Harmonizing 2 Chronicles with 1–2 Kings** — the Chronicler's version often differs; render as the Chronicler gives it.
- **The edict of Cyrus** — must be verbatim identical with Ezra 1:1–3.
