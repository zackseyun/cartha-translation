# Philippians — book context for review

- **Source**: Nestle-Aland 28 / SBL Greek NT. 4 chapters.
- **Form**: Pauline letter — warm, personal, from prison. Known as the "epistle of joy."
- **Date**: ca. 61 AD (Roman imprisonment) or earlier (Ephesian or Caesarean imprisonment).
- **Original audience**: The church at Philippi — Paul's first European church, with whom he has a strong personal bond.

## Key translation issues

1. **The Christ Hymn** (2:6–11 — carmen Christi): A likely pre-Pauline hymn. Render as poetry, formatted in lines. Key terms:
   - **μορφή** (2:6–7 — "form"): "who, being in the form of God… taking the form of a servant." "Form" — not "nature" (which is paraphrase). The word implies visible appearance/expression of essential being.
   - **ἁρπαγμός** (2:6 — "equality with God"): "Did not consider equality with God a thing to be grasped." The hapax ἁρπαγμός can mean "a thing to be seized" (res rapienda) or "a prize to be held onto" (res rapta). Footnote both options; "something to be grasped at" is defensible.
   - **ἐκένωσεν** (2:7 — "emptied himself"): The kenosis. Keep "emptied himself" — not "made himself nothing" (too vague) or "set aside his glory" (too interpretive).
   - **ὑπερυψόω** (2:9 — "highly exalted"): The compound verb is emphatic — "God highly exalted him." Keep the intensifier.
2. **χαρά / χαίρω** ("joy / rejoice"): Paul uses these throughout. Count and render consistently — "rejoice" (not "be glad" or "be happy").
3. **εἰρήνη τοῦ θεοῦ** (4:7 — "the peace of God"): "The peace of God which surpasses all understanding." Keep "surpasses all understanding" — not "passes all understanding" (archaic) or "is beyond comprehension."
4. **ἰσχύω** (4:13 — "I can do all things through him who strengthens me"): One of the most quoted verses. "All things" — not "everything." "Strengthens me" — not "gives me strength."
5. **κοινωνία** (1:5 — "partnership / fellowship / participation"): "Your partnership in the gospel." κοινωνία is rich — sharing, participation, fellowship. "Partnership" emphasizes the active cooperative dimension; "fellowship" emphasizes the relational. Context governs.

## Style / register

- Warm, affectionate, personal. Paul's most intimate letter.
- The Christ Hymn (2:6–11) is formal and elevated — contrasts with the conversational tone around it.
- Practical exhortations (4:4–9) are crisp and memorable.

## Known pitfalls

- **Rendering ἐκένωσεν as "made himself nothing"** (NIV) — this loses the specific "emptying" concept (kenosis).
- **Formatting the Christ Hymn as prose** — it is poetry and should be formatted accordingly.
- **Losing the joy vocabulary** by varying "rejoice" to "be glad," "be happy," etc.
