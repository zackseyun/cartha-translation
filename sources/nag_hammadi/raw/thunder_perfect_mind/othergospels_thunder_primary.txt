The Thunder, Perfect Mind

Introduction
**1.** I was sent forth from [the] Power,
	and have come to those who meditate on me,
	and have been found among those who seek for me.
**2.** Look at me, you who meditate on me,
	and you obedient ones, {obey me:1}.
**3.** You who {expectantly wait:2} for me, embrace me,
	and send me not away from your sight,
	and make not your voice hateful to me, nor your hearing.
**4.** Be not ignorant of me in any place or at any time.
**5.** Be on your guard; be not ignorant of me

[1] Literally, “you hearing ones . . . hear me.”
Like the Biblical “hear/hearken,” the sense is “obey.”
[2] The Coptic, like its Biblical cognates, can mean both “wait” and “expect.”
I have combined both meanings.

	and hate those who {love me?:1}
**2.** You who deny me confess me,

**3.** You who speak the truth about me lie about me,
	and you who lie about me speak the truth about me.
**4.** You who know me, be ignorant of me,
	and those who have known me not, let them know me,
	for I am knowledge and ignorance.
**5.** I am shame and pride.
**6.** I am without shame; I am ashamed.
**7.** I am strength and I am fear.
**8.** I am war and peace.
**9.** Heed me: I am the disgraced and the great.
**10.** Heed my poverty and my wealth.
**11.** Be not arrogant toward me when I am cast down on the ground,
	[and] you will find me in [those who] will come to be.
**12.** And when you look [at] me on the dung-heap,

	and you will find me in the kingdoms.
**13.** And do not despise me when I am cast out among the disgraced,
	and when in the lowliest places, laugh not at me,
	and cast me not out among those killed in violence.

Why Do You Hate Me?
**1.** I, however, I am merciful and I am cruel.
**2.** Be on your guard!
**3.** Hate not my obedience and love not my self-control.
**4.** When I am weak, forsake me not,
	and fear me not when I am in my power.
**5.** For why would you despise my fear and curse my pride?
**6.** I, however, am she who is found in all fears,
	and I am strength in trembling.
**7.** I am she who is {sick:1},
	and I am well in a pleasant place.
**8.** I am without sense and I am wise.
**9.** Why have you hated me in your counsels?
**10.** For among the silent I shall be silent,
	and I shall appear and speak.

Footnotes:
[1] literally “weak,” but the contrast to “well” indicates the meaning here refers to sickness.

Heed His Words
**1.** As [the Lord], the great power, lives,
	[he who stands] will not change the name.
**2.** [{He who stands:1}] created me,
	and I will speak his name.
**3.** Look then at his words
	and at all the writings that have been completed.
**4.** Heed them then, you obedient ones,
	and you also, you angels who have been sent,
	and you spirits who have risen from among the dead.
**5.** For I alone exist,
	and I have no one who will judge me.
**6.** For many are the fair forms of sins,
	and uncontrolled deeds, and disgraceful desires,
	and fleeting pleasures people embrace
	until they become sober
	and ascend to their place of repose.
**7.** And they will find me there,
	and they will live,
	and never die again.
