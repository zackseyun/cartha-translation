46. Sed et [de] quibus erat oratio mea] It is difficult to decide between the various possible combinations of the Lat. words in our MS, but de quibus (or de his) seems to be the simplest emendation.
Ewald’s restoration: Doch meine frage an dich ist
die, is based on the Aeth.; while the Lat., the Arab.
and also the Syr. (comp. [⋯ illegible] vi. 102, 106
(36)), require the noun in the Gk. to be δέησις rather
than ἐρώτησις. Perhaps an orig. of the form: ἀλλὰ
καὶ περὶ ὧν (or περὶ τούτων) ἡ δέησίς μου ἦν would best
account for this divergence in translation, together
with the difference in tense. The same sentiment
reappears in chap. viii. 17. Comp. Rom. x. 1.

47. quoniam ad paucos pertinebit] From the
Syr. vers. we may restore the original Greek thus:
ἀπ’ ὀλίγους μὲν μελλήσει ὁ αἰὼν ὁ ἐρχόμενος εὐφροσύνην
ποιεῖν, πολλοῖς δὲ πόνους. It is evident that the
Latin translator read μελλήσει pertinebit, for μελλήσει
incipiet; the effects of this error extend to the end
of the sentence. I have altered enim to autem;
these particles are frequently interchanged in MSS.,
e.g. in chap. vi. 18, our MS. has autem, Cod. S. enim.

48. Increuit enim in nos cor malum] For this
means after in comp. et in pectus meum increcebat
sapientia, chap. xiv. 40, Cod. S. and Rösch, p. 410.
