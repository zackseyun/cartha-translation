[FOOTNOTES]
66. Multum enim melius] In like manner our
MS. has multum (for multo) plus uae hu, chap. xiii.
13. This use of ‘multum’ with a comparative, not
unknown in classical authors, as Plaut. Most. iii. 2.
137, Cic. Off. iii. 13 (35) (in some MSS.), Stat. Theb.
ix. 559, Sil. Ital. xiii. 703, Juv. x. 197, Quint. Instit.
x. 1. 94, is of rare occurrence in biblical MSS. Ac-
cording to Vercellone, some authorities have multum
as a variant in Ruth iv. 15, et multo tibi melior est.
The same construction in Gk. is more familiar from
Homer downwards; comp. also 4 Macc. i. 3, ii. 6,
2 Cor. viii. 22, 1 Pet. i. 7 (text. rec.), and πολλῷ (text.
rec., πολλῷ μᾶλλον Heb. iii. 9, 25. This should not
be confounded with πολὺ μᾶλλον*) in Num. xxi. 12,
Deut. ix. 14, which is an attempt to represent the
Hebr. idiom for the comparative of the adj. See
Is. liv. 1, LXX.

68. conmixti sunt iniquitatibus] Perhaps συν-
εφύρησεν εἰσὶν ἀνομίαις; at least there is a strong
probability that some form of συμφύρω stood in the
original of this clause, for the [⋯ crop] of the
Syr. (comp. the Syro-flex. of Is. xlv. 19, Lam. iv. 14
Sym., Ezek. xvi. 6, 22, Job vii. 5, xxx. 14, xxxi. 30,
Jer. iii. 2), and the [⋯ crop] (=συμφυράω) of
the Arab, both point in this direction. The Gk.
word in this context may be illustrated by συμφυρό-
μενον ἐν ταῖς ἁμαρτίαις αὐτοῦ, Ecclus. xli. 14; συν-
φυρῶσιν τοιγαρᾶς, Herm. Past. Vis. ii. 2; καὶ ταῖς
συμπραγείαις συν ἀνανευρῶν ταῖς πονηραῖς, id. Vis.
ii. 3; and συμφύρεσθαι τῷ τῆς πονηρίας αὐτῶν βορ-
βόρῳ, Eus. Hist. Eccl. vii. 7. 2. For the Syr.
comp. [⋯ crop], Apocal. Bar. 21.

grauidi delictis] In the transl. from the Syr.
vers. [⋯ crop] (pl. of [⋯ crop], chap. iii. 1,
see Amira, Gram. Syr. p. 92) should not be ren-
dered ruinae, which is its proper meaning in Apocal.
Bar. 35, but delicta, both here and in chap. vii. 23.
The Syr. word is equivalent not only to πτῶσις, Is.
xvii. 1, lx. 17 (Hex.), and to πτῶμα, Job xv. 23, xvi. 14,
(Hex.), but also to παράπτωμα, Job xxxv. 15 (Hex.),
Sap. iii. 13, x. 1 (Pesh.).

69. in iudicio uenientes] Instead of in iudicio
ueni. See Rönsch, p. 406. The MS. has uenientis
here, and inspirationis (pr.m.) v. 50; other instances
of this old spelling of the pur. are given above, p. 13,
uenisset, in the sense of ueniret; it is possible,
however, that the first three letters are merely echoed
from the previous clause, and that the true read-
ing is fuisset, which satisfies the other versions.

70. cum eo] According to the Syr., Æth., and Arab.,
e.c.o. Comp. ex eo, iii. 21, vi. 54, vii. 118 (48), A. and S.

71. sensus is omitted in the MS. after crescit,
and the corrector in perplexity has inserted rel
sensum after intellege.
