## Apparatus + marker fidelity

The OCR preserved all of the following auxiliary markers Charles
1895 prints in the body text:

- **Ge'ez verse numerals in the right margin** (`፩` through `፭`
  visible on this page). These sit at the END of the text line where
  each verse starts, consistent with Charles's layout.
- **Superscript footnote references** (`²` through `³¹`). These are
  apparatus-variant pointers; the actual variant readings appear in
  a separate apparatus block at the bottom of the page, which our
  prompt correctly skips.
- **Apparatus variant-scope markers** `*` and `'`. Charles uses
  these to delimit the exact text span for which a variant reading
  is recorded. Our OCR captured them verbatim in the main text.

The English/Latin apparatus apparatus at the bottom of the page
(starting `B prefaces with... C D add...`) was correctly skipped.

## Track 3: search for a separate PD Ge'ez source

Confirmed negative. Known Ge'ez Jubilees editions:

- **Charles 1895** — our vendored PD primary. The first full critical
  Ge'ez edition.
- **Dillmann 1850-51** — German translation only, no Ge'ez critical
  edition of Jubilees specifically (Dillmann's Ge'ez work was
  Enoch). His Jubilees work in Dillmann/Rönsch 1874 is German.
- **Berger 1998** — modern critical edition, commercial (Zone 2).
- **VanderKam 1989 CSCO** — modern critical edition, commercial
  (Zone 2).
- **Beta maṣāḥǝft digital corpus** — includes many Ge'ez texts; does
  NOT include Jubilees as of 2026-04-22.
- **Ethiopian Orthodox liturgical publications** — copyright status
  per-publication; none identified under CC-BY-compatible terms.

No second independent PD Ge'ez Jubilees source exists to vendor.
The cross-validation methodology for Jubilees therefore relies on:

1. **Run-to-run consistency** (Track 2): 100% with 3.1 Pro.
2. **Content-match against Charles 1902 English** (Track 1): ✓ for
   1:1-5.
3. **Charles 1895's own internal MS apparatus** (MSS A, B, C, D
   variants in footnotes): available per-page but not systematically
   ingested yet — future work.
